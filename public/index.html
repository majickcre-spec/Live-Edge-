The Process

From Forest to Finished Work

01
Source

We work exclusively with downed, storm-fallen, or salvaged timber from Northern California. Land stewards and foresters contact us when significant trees become available. We never harvest standing trees.

02
Mill

Wood is transported to our mill partner in McCloud, at the base of Mount Shasta, where slabs are cut and air-dried for a minimum of one year. We know every log we work with by name.

03
Build

Every piece is built in our Northern California atelier. No assembly line. No CNC shortcuts on surfaces that matter. The finish is hand-rubbed. The joinery is tight. The process is slow.

The Story

A Two-Century
Old Tree.

This console began as a black oak that had stood in the Shasta foothills for over 200 years before it fell in a winter storm. The grain tells that story in quiet detail — tight, complex, marked by decades of cold seasons and hot summers. We built the base to disappear, so the wood could speak.

Black oak (Quercus kelloggii) is native to the Sierra Nevada and coastal ranges. It is rarely milled commercially — most goes to firewood. We source exclusively from downed or storm-fallen trees, working with land stewards in Shasta and Trinity counties.

The surface is finished in a natural hardwax oil, hand-rubbed in three coats. No film-forming finish. The wood breathes. The surface ages with the piece.

Care & Maintenance

Dust with a soft, dry cloth. For spills, wipe immediately with a slightly damp cloth, then dry. Re-oil annually with a natural hardwax oil. Avoid prolonged direct sunlight. Wood is a living material — movement and patina are features, not flaws.

Who We Are

The Live Edge is a furniture and sculptural art studio operating between the forests of Northern California and the McCloud mill at the base of Mount Shasta. We make heirloom-grade work from wood that has already lived its first life — then we give it a second one.

The Maker

Made by hand.
Informed by place.

The work begins with a deep knowledge of Northern California timber — its species, its character, its provenance. We spend as much time in the forest, talking to land stewards and mill operators, as we do in the workshop. The two things are inseparable.

Each piece is designed around the wood, not the other way around. We don't force a slab into a predetermined form. We find the form the material already wants to be, then we build toward it — carefully, without rush.

Every piece of furniture, every sculptural work, every luminous installation that leaves the atelier is signed, documented, and built to be passed down.

The Place

Mount Shasta.
14,179 feet.
The source of everything.

The mountain shapes every part of what we do. The forests that grow in its shadow produce timber unlike anything from a plantation. The cold air at elevation slows growth and tightens grain. The volcanic soil leaves its mark in the color and density of the wood.

McCloud sits at the mountain's base — an old mill town with deep roots in California timber history. It's where the logs become lumber, and where we first see what a piece might become.

What We Believe

The Principles Behind the Work

Provenance
Every Log Has a Story

We document the origin of every piece of wood we use — the forest, the tree, the year it fell, the log ID. That record travels with the finished piece as a certificate.

Craft
No Shortcuts on the Surface

Low volume is not a limitation — it is a commitment. Every piece receives the time it needs. The finish is hand-rubbed. The joinery is cut by hand where it counts.

Nature Elevated
The Material Is the Design

We design around what the wood wants to be. Pattern, color, and form come from the material first. The maker's job is to find them and get out of the way.

Collectibility
Built to Be Passed Down

Each major piece is registered in our archive, numbered, and accompanied by a certificate of authenticity. The work is not decoration. It is an artifact.
